MEMORIES

TOURISTS SPOT

Eiffel Tower, Paris

he Eiffel Tower is an iconic symbol of Paris and France, standing 324 meters tall. It was built in 1889 as the entrance arch to the World's Fair, and is now one of the most visited tourist attractions in the world.

Machu Picchu, Peru

Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel located in the Andes Mountains in Peru. It was built in the classical Inca style and abandoned after the Spanish conquest, only to be rediscovered in 1911. Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist destination.

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications made of stone, brick, tamped earth, and other materials, generally built along an east-to-west line across the historical northern borders of China. It was built to protect China from invasions and spans over 13,000 miles. It is now a popular tourist destination and UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Grand Canyon, USA -

The Grand Canyon is a steep-sided canyon carved by the Colorado River in Arizona, USA. It is one of the most visited national parks in the United States, attracting millions of visitors each year who come to admire its natural beauty and stunning vistas

Petra, Jordan

Petra is an ancient city located in the southwestern desert of Jordan. It was carved into the sandstone cliffs by the Nabataeans over 2000 years ago and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is known for its elaborate rock-cut architecture and water conduit system

Taj Mahal, India

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India. It was built in the 17th century by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It is widely regarded as one of the most beautiful buildings in the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
